📍 Yamba, NSW
Our chosen destination · Clarence Valley · 19 Apr – 3 May 2026
🌊 Why Yamba
Consistently rated one of Australia's best small coastal towns — and one of the last that hasn't been overrun by high rises, big resorts or fast food. At the mouth of the Clarence River where it meets the Pacific, Yamba has world-class surf, stunning beaches, great food, and a genuinely laid-back pace.

Less touristy than Byron Bay (only 90 minutes north) means April pricing is much friendlier — and the vibe is all the better for it.
⭐ Our destination Best value World-class surf Less touristy than Byron 1hr from Ballina Airport
🏖️ Beaches
Pippi Beach — Redgate's front door. 1.6km of golden sand, surf breaks off Lovers Point Rock Wall. Dog-friendly at the southern end. The one we'll wake up to.
Main Beach + Ocean Pool — Protected ocean pool right below the Pacific Hotel. Perfect for laps or a wave-free swim. Two rock pools that change with the tides.
Turners Beach — Great surf break with a breakwall walk. Farmers Market in the carpark on Wednesday mornings.
Convent Beach — Quieter, more secluded. Local secret — fewer crowds than Pippi.
Whiting Beach — Flat, calm inlet water. Ideal for kayaking and SUP. 10 min walk from town.
🏄 Angourie — 8 min drive
The surfing hamlet that launched a thousand pilgrimages. A National Surfing Reserve since 2007 — one of the most celebrated point breaks in Australia.
Angourie Point — World-famous right-hand point break. Serious surfers only at the peak, but fun for intermediate surfers on smaller days.
Blue & Green Pools — Crystal-clear natural rock pools fed by freshwater springs. Local kids climb the cliffs and dive in. One of the best rock pools in Australia — no photos do them justice.
Angourie Walking Track — 10km return from Angourie Beach to Shelly Beach Campground through Yuraygir National Park. 3–4 hours, stunning coastal scenery.
Spooky Beach — Secluded cove, excellent snorkelling. More sheltered than the main point.
🍺 Food, drink & nightlife
Pacific Hotel — The iconic Yamba pub. Ocean views from the dining room, great food, live music. Best sunset drink in town — step out of the ocean pool and walk straight up the stairs.
Wobbly Chook Brewery — Local microbrewery in the industrial estate. Lager, IPA, and the legendary 'Dirty Bird' brown ale. Worth a pilgrimage.
Clarence River Fishermen's Co-op — Famous Yamba prawns and fresh oysters straight off the boats. A seafood picnic on the breakwall is a Yamba rite of passage.
Beachwood Café — Local favourite for breakfast. Middle Eastern and Mediterranean influences, locally grown produce. Closed Mondays.
Sandbar Yamba — Beachside bistro, freshest local seafood. Great for a group lunch.
Wednesday Farmers Market — Weekly market at the Turners/Whiting Beach carpark, 7–11am. Local produce, sourdough, meats, artisan goods. Perfect start to a midweek morning.
🚣 On the water
Clarence River Ferry to Iluka — Historic MV Mirigini makes the scenic daily run across the mouth of the Clarence ($8pp). Iluka has a UNESCO World Heritage-listed rainforest — entirely free to walk through.
Clarence River History Cruise — 4hr cruise Wed departing Yamba 11am, returns 3pm. $25pp. Relaxed way to see the waterways.
Yamba Kayak — Guided moonlight paddles and day sessions on the river. Very popular — book ahead.
Go Fish Yamba — Fishing charters on the Clarence. Highly reviewed — good option for a group activity day.
SUP & kayak hire — Available in town. The flat water at Whiting Beach is ideal. Dolphins often spotted in the river.
🗺️ Day trips from Yamba
Brooms Head — 40 min south. Rocky headland, shallow rock pools at low tide, wild camping with hundreds of kangaroos. Great for fishing and swimming. Rugged and unspoilt.
Maclean — 30 min inland. Charming riverside Scottish heritage town on the Clarence. Drive up to the lookout for panoramic views across the valley and river bends.
Clarence Valley drive — Harwood Bridge (longest timber bridge in Australia), Harwood Pub on the river, cane fields and macadamia plantations, Sanctus Brewing Co at Townsend. Good half-day loop.
Byron Bay — 1.5hrs north. The famous one. Worth a day trip if you haven't been, or even if you have. Markets, lighthouse walk, pass through Brunswick Heads on the way.
Yamba Lighthouse (Pilot Hill) — 10 min walk from town. Built 1880. Best panoramic views of the coastline, ocean and Clarence River mouth. Perfect sunrise spot.
🌅 Other things to do
Bike to Angourie — Bike Shop Yamba rents bikes. 8 min ride along the highway on a dedicated bike path to Angourie. Great way to get to the pools.
Breakwall walk — Walk along the breakwall from Turners Beach to the mouth of the Clarence. Ocean on one side, river on the other.
Yamba Golf & Country Club — River Street. 18 holes with views. Green fees from $12 for members, social rates available. The Deck Café is good for breakfast.
Sunrise yoga with Salty Gypsy — Morning yoga sessions on Yamba Beach. Surf lessons also available.
Dolphins, turtles & whales — Dolphins are regularly spotted in the river and off the beaches. April is the tail end of whale migration season — keep an eye out from the lighthouse or Lovers Point.

🍺 Friday night — The Rocks pub crawl (Jess's pick)
The Rocks is Sydney's historic district — cobblestone laneways, artisan shops, museums, and some of Australia's oldest pubs. A great shout for Friday night or Saturday night depending on what fits everyone's plans.
1. Arrive early · wander The Rocks
Get there an hour or two before to explore the cobblestone laneways and soak up the atmosphere
2. Harbour View Hotel · pre-drinks
Stunning views right under the Sydney Harbour Bridge. One of the best spots in Sydney for a pre-dinner drink
3. The Glenmore · rooftop dinner
Incredible rooftop bar with views of the Opera House, Harbour Bridge and the Sydney skyline. Perfect for dinner if the weather plays ball
4. The Aussie · craft beers
Right next door to The Glenmore — 130 Australian craft beers to work through
5. Fortune of War · nightcap
Sydney's oldest pub. A fitting end to a night in the historic district
📖 Full guide to The Rocks via A Globe Well Travelled
🌊 Saturday — things to do
🚢 Manly Ferry + beach + cliff walks (Monet's pick)
Take the ferry from Circular Quay to Manly — the ferry ride itself has gorgeous harbour views and a wonderful sunset on the way back. Wander the wharf and beach, do the cliff walks around the headland, and check out the breweries and restaurants near the wharf. Less touristy than Bondi and more fun to get to.
🚶 Bondi to Bronte (or Coogee) coastal walk
The classic. 2km cliff-top walk from Bondi with tidal pools, cafes and great views. Extend it all the way to Coogee (~6km) if you've got the energy. The Icebergs pool at Bondi is iconic.
🌉 Harbour Bridge + Circular Quay
Walk the Pylon Lookout for views of the harbour and Opera House. Or go all out with BridgeClimb (~$200–270pp, book ahead). Ferry to Manly ties in nicely from here.
🍸 Saturday night — Darlinghurst on the doorstep
We're already based in Darlinghurst. Oxford St, Crown St, Stanley St all nearby — some of the best bars and restaurants in Sydney. Or do The Rocks crawl on Saturday night instead if that fits better.
